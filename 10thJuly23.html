Consumer DEflation in China

CPI fell by 0.2% in June 2023 (driven by 7.2% drop in Pork prices).
Q2 GDP grew by 6.3% compared to 2022, an underwhelming statistic as the expectation was 7.3%. This was a quarter upon quarter increase of 0.8%.
Annual CPI growth hits 0%.
Liu Guoqiang, deputy governor of the PBOC shrugged off inflation concerns. Chinese banks issued 1.81tn Yuan in June (22% increase from May). This suggests consumers are not yet feared by the threat as deflation increases the real value of debt, subsequently making it harder to repay. It is expected that China will cut interest rates, amid concerns of them missing their 5% annual GDP Growth target in 2023.
